🚨STOP! You've Been Phished!🚨

If you clicked the link in that email to reach this page, you've just experienced a phishing attack!

⚠️ What Just Happened?

This was a simulated phishing attack for educational purposes. The email you received was designed to trick you into clicking a suspicious link.

Red flags you should have noticed:

  • Unexpected email from an unknown sender
  • Suspicious or generic greeting ("Dear User" instead of your name)
  • Urgency or pressure to click immediately
  • Promise of rewards or prizes
  • Unusual or mismatched URLs
  • Poor grammar or spelling

📚 Key Lessons

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attack methods. Attackers use deceptive emails to:

  • Steal passwords and personal information
  • Install malware or ransomware
  • Access sensitive company data
  • Commit identity theft or fraud

🛡️ How to Stay Safe

  • Verify the sender: Check the email address carefully (not just the display name)
  • Hover over links: Before clicking, hover to see the actual URL
  • Never enter sensitive info: Legitimate companies won't ask for passwords via email
  • Be skeptical: If it seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • Report suspicious emails: Mark as phishing/spam and report to IT
  • Enable 2FA: Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security
