🎣 Looks like you took the bait from "Fishing Prizes" — and no, we're not talking about the kind where you catch dinner! (Get it? Phishing? 😄)

⚠️ What Just Happened?

This was a simulated phishing attack for educational purposes. The email you received was designed to trick you into clicking a suspicious link by promising a free gift card or prize.

Red flags you should have noticed:

  • Unexpected email from "Fishing Prizes" (a suspicious sender)
  • Too-good-to-be-true offer: free gift card or prize
  • Urgent language pressuring you to claim your reward NOW
  • Generic greeting ("Dear User" instead of your name)
  • Suspicious or mismatched URLs
  • Poor grammar or unusual formatting
  • Requesting you to "verify" or "confirm" personal information

📚 Key Lessons

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attack methods. Attackers use deceptive emails (like our "Fishing Prizes" scam) to:

  • Steal passwords and personal information
  • Trick you into downloading malware or ransomware
  • Access sensitive company data
  • Commit identity theft or financial fraud
  • Gain unauthorized access to accounts

Remember: Legitimate companies (like real gift card providers) will never ask you to claim a prize by clicking a random link in an email!

🛡️ How to Stay Safe & Avoid Getting "Caught"

  • Verify the sender: Check the actual email address carefully (not just the display name)
  • Hover over links: Before clicking, hover to see the actual URL destination
  • Be skeptical of "free" offers: If it seems too good to be true, it probably is
  • Never enter sensitive info: Legitimate companies won't ask for passwords, credit cards, or SSN via email
  • Look for security indicators: Check for HTTPS, official logos, and professional formatting
  • Report suspicious emails: Mark as phishing/spam and report to your IT security team
  • Enable 2FA: Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security
  • When in doubt, ask: Contact the company directly through their official website or phone number
